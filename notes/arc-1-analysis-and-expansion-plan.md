## What "The Public Interest" Does For The Story

### Thematic Function:
- **Establishes Forest's contempt for institutions** as practical strategy, not just philosophy
- **Introduces Autumn's role as technology gatekeeper** - she will control what humanity learns and how
- **Sets up the research project** that becomes cover for the actual work
- **Demonstrates Forest's manipulation skills** - he can play political theater while maintaining his principles

### Narrative Function:
- **Bridges personal survival (Ch 1-8) to community defense (later arcs)**
- **Introduces external actors** who will become important: bureaucrats, scientists, corporate interests
- **Establishes stakes** beyond Forest's valley - this is about humanity's technological development
- **Creates the framework** for Forest's institutional warfare later

### Character Function:
- **Sophia Hachia** (hot blonde bureaucrat) - control-oriented, predatory
- **Corporate representatives** (Thompson, Walsh, Park) - extraction mindset
- **Scientists** (Kellerman, Morrison, Singh, Chen) - potentially co-optable through access to "nerd porn"
- **Trix as intelligence gatherer** - hospitality as information extraction

### What Works:
- Opening 150 lines are excellent: atmospheric, character-driven, dialogue sharp
- Delegation scene is well-constructed social theater
- Forest's strategy (control information flow, gate technology) is brilliantly articulated
- Autumn's role expansion is organic and earned

### What Fails:
- **Final 1,000+ words collapse into summary**
- **Voice inconsistency** (shifts to first-person casual: "I don't think they'd even thought about zombie worms until I turned up")
- **Dramatic potential wasted**: Macintyre bull infection, hive destructions, Janna's auction—all reported rather than lived
- **Pacing breaks**: Goes from scene to bullet points

## Quality Gates

Each new/expanded chapter must pass **The Fountain Scene Test** (Chapter 02 standard):

1. **Does it establish character?** (Not tell, show through action and dialogue)
2. **Does it advance plot?** (Each scene moves story forward)
3. **Does it embody theme?** (Actions demonstrate philosophy)
4. **Does it work structurally?** (Pacing, scene density, emotional beats)
5. **Does it work emotionally?** (Reader experiences, not observes)

**Specific metrics**:
- **No summary narration** in final 20% of chapter
- **Consistent voice** throughout (third-person past, character-focused)
- **Specific sensory detail** (Queensland setting, smells, sounds, textures)
- **Dialogue that reveals character** while advancing plot
- **Technical detail embedded in experience** (not exposition)

## Notes for Drafting

### Voice Consistency
- **Third-person past tense** throughout
- **Character-focused narration** (limited POV, Forest's perspective)
- **No first-person casual** shifts
- **No bullet-point summaries** in narrative text

### Setting Work Required
**Queensland specifics needed**:
- Tree species (ironbark, blackbutt, stringybark, eucalyptus)
- Animal life (kookaburras, wallabies, leeches, cane toads)
- Weather (afternoon heat, rain, dust)
- Geography (valley, creek, granite outcrops)

### Character Voice
**Forest**:
- Laconic, dry humor
- Technical precision
- Philosophical without being preachy
- Competent without arrogance
- Short-term memory issues (needs reminding)

**Trix**:
- Practical, grounded
- Māori heritage informs worldview
- Hospitality as intelligence gathering
- Competence through action, not words

**Autumn**:
- Jen Taylor voice (Cortana)
- Enthusiastic about learning/research
- Growing personality, not fixed
- Sometimes manipulative, always strategic

**Oleksiy**:
- Laconic to extreme
- Deaf (left ear), reads lips
- History implied, never dwelt on
- Does things properly even when no one watches

### Pacing Notes
- **Action scenes**: Sharp, immediate, sensory
- **Character moments**: Slow down, let breathing happen
- **Exposition**: Embed in action or dialogue, never standalone
- **Transitions**: Let scenes breathe, don't rush
